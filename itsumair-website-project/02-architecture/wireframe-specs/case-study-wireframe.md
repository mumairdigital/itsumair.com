# Case Study Wireframe Specification

Case studies are the highest-converting asset on the site. Structure them as stories with data.

---

## Section 1 — Hero
- Breadcrumb: Home > Results > [Client]
- H1: outcome-led, e.g. "How [Client] Went From Page 3 to the Map Pack in 5 Months"
- Headline metric displayed prominently
- Hero image

## Section 2 — Client snapshot
- Business type, market, size, services used
- Compact card or table format

## Section 3 — The problem
- H2: "The Problem"
- What was broken, with data
- 2–3 paragraphs

## Section 4 — What I found
- H2: "What the Audit Found"
- Specific findings, bulleted
- This section demonstrates expertise more than any other. Be specific and technical enough to be credible, plain enough to be readable.

## Section 5 — What I built
- H2: "What I Did"
- The actual work, organized by workstream
- Before and after screenshots

## Section 6 — Results
- H2: "The Results"
- Before → after table or metric blocks
- Include timeframe. Results without a timeframe read as inflated.
- Chart or graph where data supports it

## Section 7 — Client quote
- Pull quote with full name, company, photo

## Section 8 — Services used
- Links to the relevant service pages

## Section 9 — CTA block

---

## Length target
800–1,200 words.

## Integrity requirements
- Every number must be real and verifiable
- Timeframes must be stated accurately
- If a client cannot be named, say "a Tampa bathroom remodeler" and explain why, rather than inventing a name
- Adjacent or pre-niche work must be labeled clearly as related work, not presented as bathroom-remodeler results
