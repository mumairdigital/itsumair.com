# Location Page Wireframe Specification

## Critical context
These pages will get very little search traffic. Their real jobs are:
1. Sales enablement — send to a prospect in that city as proof you know his market
2. Local relevance signals
3. Long-tail capture
4. Demonstration of the exact page type you would build for a client

**Each page must contain genuinely unique local research or it fails all four jobs.** 60% unique content minimum, or do not publish.

---

## Section 1 — Hero
- Breadcrumb: Home > Areas > [City]
- H1: `Local SEO for Bathroom Remodelers in [City], FL`
- Subhead: one sentence with a specific local fact
- Primary CTA

## Section 2 — Local market snapshot
- H2: "The [City] Bathroom Remodeling Market"
- Real data: housing stock age, demographics, why bathroom demand exists here
- 2–3 paragraphs
- This is the section that makes the page credible. Do the research.

## Section 3 — Local competitive picture
- H2: "What It Takes to Rank in [City]"
- How many bathroom remodelers compete locally
- What their GBP profiles and review counts look like
- Who currently wins the Map Pack and why
- This demonstrates real analysis and is genuinely useful to the prospect

## Section 4 — Neighborhoods served
- H2: "Neighborhoods and Areas I Cover"
- Real named neighborhoods, not invented ones
- Short list or grid

## Section 5 — Relevant proof
- Case study card or a local example
- Link to full case study

## Section 6 — Services in [City]
- Brief list linking to the 4 service pages

## Section 7 — City FAQ
- 2–3 questions specific to this city

## Section 8 — Nearby areas
- Links to 2–3 geographic neighbors only

## Section 9 — CTA block

---

## Length target
700–1,000 words of genuinely unique content.

## Image guidance
**Do not use generic stock city skylines.** They add nothing and look templated. Use a simple branded service-area map graphic (reused across pages with the relevant city highlighted). A clean image-light location page beats a stock-photo one.
