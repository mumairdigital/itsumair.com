# Risk Register

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | Site built for homeowner keywords by mistake | Medium | Critical | Stated at the top of README and every content brief; QA check before launch |
| R2 | No Tampa case studies at launch undermines credibility | High | High | Use honest adjacent proof; prioritize first client for a documented case study; publish teardowns |
| R3 | Thin content penalty from location pages | Medium | High | Launch only 6, each with genuinely unique local research, 700+ words |
| R4 | Prospect distrust due to overseas location | High | High | Address directly in FAQ and About; US phone number; fast response SLA; strong proof |
| R5 | Low search volume means little organic traffic | Certain | Medium | Expected and planned for — site is a conversion asset; traffic comes from outreach |
| R6 | Accessibility complaint or demand letter | Low | High | WCAG 2.1 AA compliance built in from the start; accessibility statement published |
| R7 | Cold outreach damages domain reputation | Medium | High | Use a separate sending domain for cold email; configure SPF/DKIM/DMARC; never send from the primary domain |
| R8 | Solo capacity — site promises more than can be delivered | Medium | Medium | Never imply a team; position founder-led service as the advantage |
| R9 | Blog abandoned after launch | High | Medium | Commit to a realistic cadence; an abandoned blog is worse than none |
| R10 | Image licensing violation | Medium | High | Never use client or competitor photography; document every license (06-assets) |
| R11 | Google Business Profile ineligibility | Certain | Medium | Do not attempt a fake Tampa listing; see 04-seo/local-seo-entity-plan.md for the compliant approach |
| R12 | Niche too narrow to fill capacity | Medium | Medium | Bathroom-first, expand to kitchen and bath only after position is established |
| R13 | Client churn due to remodeling seasonality | Medium | Medium | Build recurring maintenance revenue; diversify client sizes |
| R14 | Losing domain or accounts | Low | Critical | 2FA everywhere, registrar lock, auto-renew, documented recovery access |

## Highest-priority risks
R1, R2, and R4 are the three that most commonly kill projects like this. R1 is prevented by discipline, R2 by honesty plus urgency in landing the first client, R4 by addressing it openly rather than hoping nobody notices.
