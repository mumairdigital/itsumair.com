# Audience Persona

## Primary persona: "Dave, the bathroom remodeler"

**Demographics**
- Owner-operator, bathroom remodeling company, Tampa Bay
- Age 45–65
- Revenue $300k–$3M
- 2–15 employees or subcontractor crews
- Often a former tradesman who built a business

**Context**
- Reads on a phone, often between jobs, often outdoors in bright light
- Checks email early morning or evening, rarely mid-day
- Is on Facebook and possibly Instagram. Rarely on LinkedIn.
- Does not read marketing blogs

**Pains**
- Invisible in the Google Map Pack while a competitor dominates
- Paying $150–$600 per shared lead to Angi/HomeAdvisor and racing three other companies to the phone
- Website built in 2016 that does not convert and cannot be edited
- No idea whether his marketing is working
- Busy season and dead season with no pipeline control

**Fears**
- Getting burned again by an agency that took the money and sent screenshots
- Being locked into a 12-month contract
- Jargon being used to hide the fact that no work happened
- Losing control of his website or Google listing

**Motivations**
- More jobs, better jobs, higher ticket
- Predictable pipeline
- Not being dependent on lead marketplaces
- Being the name people know locally

**Decision criteria**
1. Proof — real results from real businesses
2. Specificity — does this person understand MY business
3. Plain speech — no jargon
4. Responsiveness — will he actually pick up the phone
5. Risk — can I get out if it does not work

**Does NOT care about**
- Tech stack, frameworks, page speed scores in isolation
- Follower counts
- Awards or badges from organizations he has not heard of
- Marketing theory

**Objections in order of frequency**
1. How much does it cost
2. How long until I see results
3. Do I have to sign a contract
4. How is this different from the last agency
5. You are not in Tampa, how does that work
6. Do I own the website
7. I already have a website
8. How do I know it is working

**Writing implication**
8th grade reading level. Short sentences. Concrete numbers. Real photographs. Zero jargon. Answer the money and contract questions before he has to ask.

## Secondary persona: office manager or marketing coordinator
At larger firms ($2M+), the first contact may be an office manager researching options. She needs: clear service descriptions, pricing signals, and something credible to forward to the owner. This is why the pricing page and case studies matter for internal selling.
