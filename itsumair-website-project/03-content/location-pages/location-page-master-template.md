# Location Page Master Template

## Read this before writing any location page

**These pages will get very little search traffic.** Their real jobs are:
1. Sales enablement — send to a prospect in that city as proof you know his market
2. Local relevance signals for the site
3. Long-tail capture
4. Demonstration of the exact page type you would build for a client

**Each page must contain genuinely unique local research or it fails all four jobs.** 60% unique content minimum. A page with the city name swapped into a template is worse than no page — it signals to a prospect that you did not bother.

## Research required per city (do this before writing)
- [ ] Housing stock age and typical build era
- [ ] Median home value
- [ ] Notable 55+ / retirement communities
- [ ] Real neighborhood names
- [ ] Count of bathroom remodelers competing locally (Google Maps)
- [ ] Review counts of the top 3 in the Map Pack
- [ ] Website quality of the top 3
- [ ] Anything genuinely distinctive about the local market

## Template structure
1. Hero — H1 `Local SEO for Bathroom Remodelers in [City], FL` + subhead with a specific local fact
2. Local market snapshot — housing age, demographics, why bathroom demand exists here
3. Local competitive picture — how many competitors, their review counts, who wins the Map Pack and why
4. Neighborhoods served — real names only
5. Relevant proof
6. Services in [City]
7. City FAQ (2–3)
8. Nearby areas (geographic neighbors only)
9. CTA block

## Word count
700–1,000 unique words

## Images
**No generic stock city skylines.** Use a branded service-area map graphic, reused across pages with the relevant city highlighted.

## Metadata pattern
- **Title:** `Bathroom Remodeler SEO in [City], FL | Umair`
- **Meta description:** local angle + service + CTA

## Schema
`Service` with `areaServed` (City). **Not `LocalBusiness`** — there is no physical presence in these cities and claiming one would be false.
