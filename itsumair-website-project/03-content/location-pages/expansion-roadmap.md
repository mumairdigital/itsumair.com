# Location Expansion Roadmap

Build these only after launch, and only as real local proof accumulates. Adding pages without proof or research repeats the thin-content risk the launch plan was designed to avoid.

## Trigger for adding a location page
Add a new location page when at least one is true:
- You have signed a client in or near that city
- You have completed a documented audit or teardown for a business there
- You have genuine research about that local market

## Expansion list (ranked)

| # | Location | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 7 | Riverview | Fast-growing south Hillsborough, newer stock but large and expanding homeowner base |
| 8 | Wesley Chapel | High-growth Pasco corridor, master-planned communities |
| 9 | Seminole | Central Pinellas, older housing, strong 55+ presence |
| 10 | Safety Harbor / Oldsmar | Affluent, mature north Pinellas |
| 11 | Tarpon Springs | Older coastal stock, notable retiree share |
| 12 | New Port Richey / Trinity | Pasco retiree concentration, aging-in-place demand |
| 13 | Carrollwood | Established affluent north Tampa, mature homes |
| 14 | Apollo Beach / Ruskin | Waterfront and growing south Hillsborough |
| 15 | Westchase / Town 'n' Country | Dense west Hillsborough suburban base |

## Deliberately excluded
- **New-construction master-planned areas** with very young housing (much of FishHawk Ranch, Lakewood Ranch). New bathrooms do not get remodeled.
- **Cities outside a realistic service radius.**
- **Any city where genuine local research cannot be produced.**

## Metro-level expansion (beyond Tampa Bay)
If Tampa Bay is saturated or capacity allows, the ranked next markets from the prior metro analysis are:
1. Sarasota / Bradenton — higher ticket, peak aging-in-place, smaller pond
2. Cape Coral / Fort Myers
3. Ocala

Do not expand to a second metro until the Tampa Bay position is established with multiple case studies.
