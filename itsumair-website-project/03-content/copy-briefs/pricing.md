# Copy Brief: Pricing

**URL:** `/pricing/`
**Word count:** 700–1,000
**Priority:** P1
**Dependent on:** Decision D3

## Rationale
Contractors quote jobs for a living. They respect published pricing and read opacity as a trick. Publishing also filters tire-kickers before they consume call time.

---

## H1
`Straightforward Pricing. No Long Contracts.`

## Sections

### 1. Hero
- H1 + subhead

### 2. Why I publish pricing
- H2: `Why I Show My Prices`
- Short honest paragraph: you quote your jobs, I quote mine. Hiding price wastes both our time.
- **This paragraph itself is a trust asset.**

### 3. Monthly plans
- H2: `Monthly Plans`
- 3 tiers, all prices "starting at" — real quotes vary with scope (see §5):
  - **Foundation** — starting at $497/month. GBP optimization + management, review generation system, basic local SEO (Map Pack fundamentals), monthly report. For remodelers starting out.
  - **Growth** — starting at $897/month. Everything in Foundation + ongoing local SEO (on-page, citations, content), competitor tracking, monthly strategy call. The typical fit. Visually emphasized.
  - **Full System** — starting at $1,497/month. Everything in Growth + website updates/maintenance included, advanced content (blog + service pages), priority turnaround.
- Each: "starting at $X/month", who it is for, bulleted inclusions, CTA

### 4. Website design
- H2: `Websites`
- Custom website build/rebuild: starting at $2,497. Price moves up based on site complexity, number of pages, and whether it's a rebuild vs. a fresh build.
- What affects the price
- Maintenance (for clients not on Full System): starting at $147/month — updates, security, uptime monitoring, small content changes

### 5. What affects your price
- H2
- Honest variables: current site condition, competition level in your area, number of service areas, review volume, how much needs fixing first

### 6. What's not included
- H2: `What's Not Included`
- Ad spend, third-party tool subscriptions, photography, anything outside scope
- **Integrity signal. Do not skip this section.**

### 7. ROI framing ← the strongest argument on the page
- H2: `What This Is Worth to You`
- Concrete math: a single bathroom remodel averages $15,000+. The Growth plan runs $897/month, or $10,764/year. If it helps close just one extra job a quarter, it has paid for itself more than five times over.
- Frame it as the cheapest lead source they have, not as a marketing expense.
- Keep it simple and honest. Do not inflate.
